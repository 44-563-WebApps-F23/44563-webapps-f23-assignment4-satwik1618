Satwik Yarlagadda acts in a play

An Enemy of the People Act 3 -"Henrik Ibsen"

Billing

That is true; and for that reason we must strike blow upon blow until the whole of this aristocracy tumbles to pieces. As I sat in there reading this, I almost seemed to see a revolution in being. [ A knock is heard at the door ]

Hovstad.

Speak so that Aslaksen cannot hear you.

Dr.Stockmann

Yes, print away. Undoubtedly it has come to that. Now they must take what they get. There is going to be a fight in the town, Mr. Billing [They show him to the door and bow him out]

Thomas

may be sure they will. And in this way the ring will be broken up, you see, and then in every issue of the paper we will enlighten the public on the Mayor's incapability on one point and another, and make it clear that all the positions of trust in the town, the whole control of municipal affairs, ought to be put in the hands of the Liberals.

Aslaksen

Yes, but in moderation, Doctor—proceed with moderation. [Goes into the printing room.]

Petra

You don't understand me. The burden of this story is that there is a supernatural power that looks after the so-called good people in this world and makes everything happen for the best in their case—while all the so-called bad people are punished.

Peter Stockmann

It is pleasing evidence of a public-spirited feeling, extremely pleasing evidence. I might almost say I hardly expected it. But you have a closer knowledge of public opinion than I.
